## ğŸ“‰ Variance ($\sigma^2$): Measuring the Spread

**Variance** measures the **spread** or dispersion of a **single** variable's data points around its **mean** (average).

* A **high variance** means the individual data points are widely spread out from the average.
* A **low variance** means the data points cluster tightly around the average.

Variance is measured in squared units of the data.



---

## ğŸ“ˆ Covariance: Measuring How Two Variables Change Together

**Covariance** measures the degree to which **two random variables change together**. It indicates the **direction** of the linear relationship, but not the strength.

* A **positive covariance** means that as one variable increases, the other tends to increase (and vice-versa). The variables move in the same direction.
* A **negative covariance** means that as one variable increases, the other tends to decrease. The variables move in opposite directions.
* A value close to **zero** means there is no clear linear relationship.
